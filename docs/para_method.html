The PARA Method

Overview

The PARA Method is a simple, flexible, and action-oriented system for organizing your digital files. It's based on the idea that you should organize your files based on their purpose and actionability, rather than just their topic.

The Four Categories

  • 1. Projects

    Things you're actively working on with a defined goal and a deadline. Examples: "Complete the website redesign," "Plan a vacation," "Finish a course."

  • 2. Areas

    The spheres of your life that require ongoing attention and maintenance. Examples: "Finances," "Health," "Personal Growth," "Work Responsibilities."

  • 3. Resources

    Topics of ongoing interest that you want to keep track of, but that aren't tied to a specific project. Examples: "Gardening tips," "Interesting articles," "Design inspiration."

  • 4. Archive

    Inactive items from the other three categories. This is where you move completed projects, areas you're no longer involved in, and resources that are no longer relevant.
